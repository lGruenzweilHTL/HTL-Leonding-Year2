History of the Web

The World Wide Web (WWW), commonly known as the Web, is an information system where documents and other web resources are identified by Uniform Resource Locators (URLs, such as https://www.example.com/), which may be interlinked by hypertext, and are accessible over the Internet. The resources of the Web are transferred via the Hypertext Transfer Protocol (HTTP) and may be accessed by users by a software application called a web browser.

  • Invention of the Web: Tim Berners-Lee developed the web at CERN to easily store, update, and distribute documents (1989).
  • Approach and Concepts:
  • Avoidance of tree structures and keywords.
  • Use of the ENQUIRE system from 1980.
  • Ted Nelson's Hypertext model as a foundation.
  • HyperCard: Apple's HyperCard was popular, but Berners-Lee's system supported multiple databases and users worldwide.
  • Extended Features:
  • Support for multimedia (graphics, video, etc.).
  • Dynamic links for mutable data.
  • Gateways for access to other systems.
  • Decentralization without central control.
